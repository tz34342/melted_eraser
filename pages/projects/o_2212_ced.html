On Perceiving Agency

University of California, Berkeley, College of Environmental Design

Special Thanks to: Peter Suen + Isabelle de Metz (instructors)

How do we capture motion? Traditional ways to represent motion relies on two references: reference to the ground (to locate the body to the world) and reference to the self (to contrast the body from a previous instance of itself). I believe, however, it is possible to represent motion with neither references.

The drawing on the left is a traditional way to represent a jumping motion. The drawing to the right is of the same jumping motion, but with neither reference to the ground nor the self: it instead showcases a possibility of movement, an agency distilled. The center of each circle represents a joint in the leg, and the circles represent possible movements that can be born out of that exact moment in time.
