Thunder on a Stick

Special Thanks to: Cal Raijin Taiko (commission)

This project was requested by Cal Raijin Taiko’s leadership for the spring 2024 semester’s end-of- the-school-year show. With the goal of bringing more color and energy to the group, the leadership specifically asked for a pair of “matoi,” which was traditionally used for Japanese fire fighting, and nowadays for festive celebrations.

I was given free-reign over the project, and several variations were considered. Ultimately, I landed on the current version of the matoi, which I believe are most representative of Raijin’s unabashed joy and explosive energy in their performances. Instead of a more traditional proportion and standard “box-like” head- banner design, a single exaggerated kanji of “thunder” was used. “Raijin” means “God of Thunder,” and thus the kanji of thunder works well as an insignia for Cal Raijin Taiko. The kanji, used in exaggeration, also creates the effect of synesthesia, or “visualized sound effect.” The directed swings of the matoi create no external audible sound on its own, but they rather invoke sounds of thunder, of explosive spirit, internally within the audience’s minds.
