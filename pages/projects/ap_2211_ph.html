Freedom, Domesticated

2023 Lyon Photography Prize, 2nd Place

There is a strange sense of freedom inside Hyatt Regency, constructed out of many contradictions. The spacious interior space of the building is an open space, but it is an enclosed, domesticated kind of open space, different from one experienced for example in an open field. The corridors are constructed out of careful calculation, with order and rigidity, yet it is not controlling of human behavior. Can the open space in the center be also interpreted as another form of exterior, of the outside, if we treat the corridors with privately enclosed residential rooms as the actual mass of the building?

And also, how do you represent such an experience through the medium of photography?
