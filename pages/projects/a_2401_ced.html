Boundless

University of California, Berkeley, College of Environmental Design

Special Thanks to: Adam Miller (instructor)

A square (or a unit grid) is a common tool for demarcation, containment, and division. Is it possible to misinterpret a boundary so explicit?

Boundless is a pavilion that encloses plant life instead of people. The square-base, along with its extruded curved walls, do not gather people, and instead rejects them, sheltering them only under the exterior curvature. From across the street, passersby only see a strong sculptural form. As they attempt to get to know the pavilion better by walking closer, they unexpectedly encounter windows of views into the pavilion, seeing the green landscape infinitely reflected by the mirrored finishes of the pavilion wallsâ€™ interior sides. Observers see a boundless landscape untouched by the scars of a square, a grid.

In this space, the square can be perceived in all ways: explicitly, vaguely, subvertingly, and perhaps even unfriendly.
